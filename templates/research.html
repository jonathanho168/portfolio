「what i study」

I'm interested in the integration of predictive analytics systems for situations requiring high sensitivity and specificity, like child welfare systems or emergency room prioritisation.

One big issue we face is preventing algorithm aversion and automation bias, which are two undesirable ends of a broad spectrum.

Most recently, I joined the 4A (AI Accountability, Awareness, and Action) Lab in the Social AI group here at Carnegie Mellon's Human-Computer Interaction Institute in the School of Computer Science.

I work closely with Seyun Kim, a second-year Ph.D. student, and Professors Haiyi Zhu, Motahhare Eslami, and John Zimmerman.

Previously, I looked at the effects of having two lenders of last resort with disclosure, which is discussed in Liquidity from two lending facilities.

I also contributed to The Scientific Method, a book on norms and recent changes in scientific practice and validity.
